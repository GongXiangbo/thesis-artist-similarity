# Related Works

## Style Anchor

Write only in polished academic English. Use the supplied Politecnico di Torino theses only as style and tone models: open the chapter with a short purpose statement, group work by research theme, explain how each group supports or motivates the thesis, and end with the gap addressed by the project. Do not reuse their wording, citations, literature claims, or examples unless the current project sources support them.

Use "Related Works" consistently unless the manuscript has already chosen "State of the Art".

## Chapter Shape

Organize by conceptual families rather than by paper chronology. Possible groups include:

1. Music Information Retrieval and artist similarity.
2. Audio-based similarity and musical feature extraction.
3. Metadata, cultural, or social-context approaches.
4. Image, text, and social media representations.
5. Multimodal fusion and embedding methods.
6. Siamese, metric-learning, or contrastive approaches.
7. Evaluation methods for similarity, retrieval, recommendation, and visualization.

Include only groups that are relevant to the actual thesis.

## Literature Paragraph Pattern

For each paragraph:

1. Introduce the research theme.
2. Summarize the main approach used by one or more cited works.
3. Explain the strength or limitation relevant to this thesis.
4. Transition toward the thesis gap.

Prefer synthesis over listing. A good paragraph compares methods, assumptions, data modalities, or evaluation criteria. Avoid one-sentence summaries of unrelated papers.

## Critical Tone

Be respectful and precise. Use guarded phrases:

- "Previous studies have mainly focused on..."
- "These approaches provide..."
- "However, they often rely on..."
- "This leaves open the question of..."
- "The present thesis addresses this gap by..."

Do not claim that prior work "fails" unless the cited evidence clearly supports that judgment. Distinguish between a limitation of a paper, a limitation of a dataset, and a limitation of a modality.

## Citation Discipline

Attach citations to specific claims. Do not cite a paper for broad statements it does not make. Do not invent bibliographic details. If a citation is missing, mark it as needing verification instead of fabricating it.

## Revision Checklist

Ensure the final paragraph makes the research gap explicit and prepares the reader for Methodology. Check that every cited work appears in the Bibliography and every bibliography entry is cited in the thesis body.
