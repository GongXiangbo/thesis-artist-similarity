# Contents

## Style Anchor

Build the contents as an engineering thesis map, not as a marketing outline. Use the supplied Politecnico di Torino theses only as structural and tonal references: a concise abstract, a contents page, a context-setting introduction, a theory chapter, a literature chapter, a method chapter, experiments and results, conclusions, and bibliography. Do not copy their research content, citations, or page numbering.

Write all headings in English. Do not add an Italian abstract or Italian section title unless the user explicitly asks for it.

## Recommended Order

Use this default order unless the project template or supervisor requires a different one:

1. Abstract
2. Contents
3. Introduction
4. Theoretical Background
5. Related Works
6. Methodology
7. Experiments and Results
8. Conclusions
9. Bibliography
10. List of Figures, List of Tables, and Acknowledgements only if required by the thesis template

## Chapter Function

Assign one job to each chapter:

- Introduction: motivate the problem, scope the thesis, state objectives and contributions, preview the document.
- Theoretical Background: define concepts needed to understand the method.
- Related Works: position the thesis against existing research and identify the gap.
- Methodology: specify the proposed system, data processing, model, training objective, and implementation choices.
- Experiments and Results: describe datasets, setups, metrics, ablations, results, interpretation, and limitations.
- Conclusions: synthesize contributions, answer the research objective, and propose future work.
- Bibliography: list only works cited in the thesis, consistently formatted.

## Heading Rules

Use Title Case for chapter titles and sentence-style capitalization for long subsection titles if that is already used in the manuscript. Keep headings short, concrete, and technical. Prefer "Experiments and Results" over vague labels such as "Evaluation" when the chapter includes both setup and findings.

Use two or three levels of hierarchy for most chapters. Avoid deep nesting unless mathematical derivations, model components, or experiments require it.

## Consistency Checks

Ensure each contents item corresponds to an actual section in the manuscript. Align terminology across chapters: do not alternate between "Related Works", "State of the Art", and "Literature Review" without a deliberate reason. Check that the contents does not promise analyses, baselines, datasets, or future work that the thesis body does not contain.
