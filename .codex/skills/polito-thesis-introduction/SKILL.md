# Introduction

## Style Anchor

Write only in polished academic English. Use the supplied Politecnico di Torino theses only as style and tone models: begin from a broad domain shift, narrow toward the thesis problem, define why the problem matters, and then state the thesis objective. Do not reuse their wording, claims, citations, numbers, or examples unless the current project sources support them.

Use a formal, explanatory voice. Prefer "this thesis focuses on...", "the objective is...", and "the proposed work investigates..." over personal narrative. Keep the introduction accessible to an examiner who knows engineering methods but may not know every model used in the project.

## Chapter Shape

Build the introduction with this progression:

1. Domain context: explain why artist similarity matters for music consumption, MIR, recommendation, discovery, or cultural analysis.
2. Technological shift: describe why digital platforms, audio data, social media, or multimodal representations change the problem.
3. Research gap: state what traditional or existing approaches do not fully capture.
4. Thesis objective: formulate the central aim as a concrete research problem.
5. Proposed direction: summarize the modalities, representation strategy, learning model, or evaluation concept at a high level.
6. Contributions: list or describe the main contributions only if they are actually delivered by the thesis.
7. Thesis organization: close with a concise chapter roadmap.

## Tone and Argument

Move from general to specific in each paragraph. Start paragraphs with clear topic sentences. Connect claims with evidence or citations when discussing the literature, and avoid unsupported statements about the music industry or AI.

Use cautious academic verbs:

- "aims to investigate"
- "seeks to assess"
- "provides a framework"
- "is motivated by"
- "can contribute to"

Avoid:

- Excessive historical background that belongs in Theoretical Background.
- Detailed architecture, hyperparameters, or metric tables that belong later.
- Overclaiming that the method solves artist similarity in general.
- Chapter summaries longer than one short paragraph.

## Contribution Framing

When writing contributions, make each item testable. Good contributions name an artifact or result: a dataset construction procedure, a multimodal representation, a trained model, an evaluation comparison, an analysis of embeddings, or a limitation discovered experimentally.

## Revision Checklist

Check that the introduction answers four questions: why this topic matters, what is missing, what this thesis does, and how the rest of the document is organized. Ensure all terminology used in the objective is later defined in the thesis.
