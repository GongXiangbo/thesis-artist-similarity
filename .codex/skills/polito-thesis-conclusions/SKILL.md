# Conclusions

## Style Anchor

Write only in polished academic English. Use the supplied Politecnico di Torino theses only as style and tone models: restate the thesis aim, synthesize the method and empirical findings, explain the contribution, acknowledge limitations, and close with realistic future work. Do not reuse their wording, claims, numbers, or future-work ideas unless the current project sources support them.

Use a confident but careful tone. The conclusion should sound earned by the experiments, not promotional.

## Chapter Shape

Use this default progression:

1. Restate the research objective in one concise paragraph.
2. Summarize the proposed approach and why it was appropriate.
3. Present the key findings, tied to reported metrics or analyses.
4. Explain the contribution to artist similarity, MIR, multimodal representation, or recommendation.
5. Discuss limitations in a constructive way.
6. Propose future work that directly follows from the limitations or results.

Do not introduce new literature, new experiments, new metrics, or new technical claims.

## Finding Synthesis

Write findings as interpretations of evidence:

- "The experiments showed that..."
- "The results suggest that..."
- "The analysis indicates that..."
- "This behavior can be attributed to..."

When results are mixed, state the tradeoff clearly. If one modality or model variant outperformed another, mention the evaluation conditions and avoid implying universal superiority.

## Limitations and Future Work

Frame limitations as scope boundaries, data constraints, model assumptions, evaluation limits, or implementation tradeoffs. Future work should be specific enough to be actionable: additional data sources, stronger baselines, alternative fusion strategies, improved labels, robustness checks, user studies, or deployment-oriented evaluation.

Avoid:

- Repeating the abstract.
- Introducing citations that are not needed for final synthesis.
- Overstating practical impact.
- Ending with a vague sentence such as "many improvements are possible".

## Revision Checklist

Ensure the conclusion answers the thesis objective and matches the actual results chapter. Check that every claimed contribution has appeared earlier in the thesis and every future direction follows from an identified limitation or observation.
